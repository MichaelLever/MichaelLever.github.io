Dr. Michael W. Lever

Assistant Professor & MHMS Program Coordinator

I am a marketing researcher advancing consumer behaviour and brand relationship theory through innovative methodologies ranging from netnography to latent class analysis. My research focuses on digital consumer engagement, brand advocacy, and service-based relationships, particularly within the tourism and hospitality sectors. With over 20 peer-reviewed publications in leading journals and more than $120,000 in research funding, including an SSHRC Partnership Engage Grant, I am dedicated to bridging academic theory with industry practice. Currently serving as Assistant Professor and MHMS Program Coordinator at Fairleigh Dickinson University's Vancouver Campus, I am passionate about student-centered learning and fostering cross-sector collaboration to advance our understanding of contemporary consumer behavior in digital environments.

Research Interests & Projects

Digital Consumer Engagement

Exploring how consumers interact with brands through social media platforms, user-generated content, and digital storytelling. Current projects examine shifts in social media behavior during global events and the role of visual content in destination marketing.

Brand Advocacy & Relationships

Investigating the psychological drivers of brand advocacy, particularly in tourism contexts. My work examines how national identification and tourism ethnocentrism influence destination advocacy behaviors among residents and visitors.

Tourism & Hospitality Innovation

Researching innovative approaches to destination marketing, including the use of AI in travel planning, accessibility in tourism, and the impact of geopolitical factors on travel behavior. Recent focus on post-pandemic recovery strategies.

Consumer Segmentation & Analytics

Applying advanced analytical techniques including latent class analysis and eye-tracking technology to understand consumer behavior patterns. Projects include typologies of travel safety perceptions and cross-border shopping behaviors.
